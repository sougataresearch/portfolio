Optical Metrology, AI Super-Resolution & Semiconductor Inspection

At NTU's Precision Metrology Lab, my doctoral work targets a fundamental challenge: measuring nanoscale structures smaller than the wavelength of light used to probe them. As transistors enter Gate-All-Around and 3D NAND regimes, metrology must advance faster than the devices themselves.

02 · Master's Thesis · Jadavpur University · 2024–2025

Lead-Free High-Entropy Perovskite Oxide Nanomaterials

A novel class of lead-free, high-entropy perovskite oxide where five or more metal cations occupy a single crystallographic phase. Configurational entropy — not stoichiometry — becomes the primary stabilizing force, enabling elemental combinations that would otherwise phase-separate.

Synthesized via the sol-gel route for atomic-level homogeneity. Phase confirmed by XRD with Rietveld refinement. Spatial homogeneity verified by FESEM/EDS mapping. Optical bandgap from UV-Vis spectroscopy; thermodynamics from TGA/DSC.

04Student Guide — For Indian Students

I went from a regular college in Paschim Medinipur to a PhD seat at National Taiwan University — without IELTS, without a famous undergraduate institution, and without knowing anyone abroad. This is everything I wish someone had told me.

India is at an inflection point. With the India Semiconductor Mission channelling billions into fabrication plants — backed by Tata Electronics, Micron, and CG Power — demand for engineers who understand both the physics and the process is growing faster than supply.

A Physics or Electronics graduate from any Indian university who commits seriously to this field has a legitimate path to global opportunities, because the knowledge itself is portable even when the institution name is not.

Specificity is everything. Open by naming a specific paper of theirs and explaining what in it connected with your work. Keep the email under 200 words. Attach a one-page CV. Do not write "Respected Sir/Madam" — use their proper title and last name. Follow up once after two weeks.

Your CV is a curated argument for why a professor should invest time in you. The most important entry is your thesis: what you did, what technique, what result. Use LaTeX to typeset it — it signals precision. List only tools you can genuinely use.

Start with the research question, not the university ranking. Use Google Scholar to find the most active researchers in your area in the last three years. Reach out to current students — they will tell you things the professor's webpage never will.

Many programs in Taiwan, South Korea, Japan, and parts of Europe waive English test requirements. NTU and NTHU waive IELTS/TOEFL if your education was in English — true for almost every Indian student. You typically just need a medium-of-instruction letter from your university's examination office.

Taiwan's Taiwan Experience Education Program (TEEP) is the most accessible fully-funded short-term research internship for Indian students in semiconductor and materials science — flight, accommodation, and a monthly stipend. My TEEP at NTHU's CoSR in 2025 led directly to my PhD at NTU.

Within India, INUP workshops at IITs and the IISc winter school at CeNSE are excellent for hands-on cleanroom experience and CV credibility.

MATLAB is the lingua franca of optical simulation and signal processing. Python with NumPy/SciPy/Matplotlib is essential for AI and data-driven work. Origin is the standard for scientific data plotting in materials science. LaTeX is non-negotiable for papers and theses. Start learning it during your M.Sc., not when the deadline arrives.
