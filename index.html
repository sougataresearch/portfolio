Profile

Background & Transition

I hold an M.Sc. in Electronics Science from Jadavpur University, following a B.Sc. in Physics. My academic training combines solid-state physics, semiconductor device fundamentals, and experimental materials characterization.

During my master’s work, I focused on synthesis and characterization of lead-free high-entropy perovskite oxides, gaining hands-on experience with XRD, FESEM/EDS, optical spectroscopy, and thermal analysis. This exposure motivated a shift from materials toward measurement-centric problems.

Through semiconductor process training and current research exposure at National Tsing Hua University, my interests have converged on semiconductor metrology—particularly optical and interferometric methods for surface and thin-film measurement.

Research

Research Interests & Questions

Optical & Interferometric Metrology

Semiconductor surface and thin-film measurement

I am interested in optical and white-light interferometric techniques for non-contact 3D surface profiling in semiconductor inspection.

Open question:
How can axial resolution and measurement robustness be maintained when profiling rough or multilayer semiconductor surfaces?

Computational Imaging & Signal Processing

Physics-based data analysis for metrology

I aim to develop data processing and modeling approaches that improve interpretation of interferometric and optical measurement data.

Open question:
What signal processing strategies best mitigate noise and phase ambiguity in high-aspect-ratio surface measurements?
