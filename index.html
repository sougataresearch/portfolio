Research

Research Interests & Open Questions

Optical and Interferometric Metrology

Optical methods are central to non-contact, high-throughput inspection in semiconductor manufacturing.

A key limitation in interferometric surface profiling is maintaining axial resolution and accuracy when measuring complex or high-aspect-ratio features.

Open question: How can interferometric surface measurements be made more robust against noise and surface discontinuities commonly found in advanced semiconductor structures?

Computational Imaging and Signal Interpretation

Computational approaches are increasingly used to extract physical meaning from optical measurement data.

Interferometric and imaging measurements often involve trade-offs between resolution, speed, and signal stability.

Open question: What signal-processing strategies can improve measurement reliability without increasing experimental complexity in optical metrology systems?

Semiconductor Surfaces and Thin-Film Characterization

Surface and thin-film properties directly influence device performance and process control.

Understanding how optical measurements correlate with material structure and morphology remains a practical challenge.

Open question: How can optical surface measurements be better linked to material properties relevant to semiconductor process monitoring?
