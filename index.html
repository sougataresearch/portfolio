01 · Master's Thesis · Jadavpur University

Lead-Free High-Entropy Perovskite Oxide Nanomaterials

The global push to eliminate toxic lead from functional materials has opened a fascinating frontier in perovskite science. For my master's thesis, I synthesized a novel class of lead-free, high-entropy perovskite oxide — a material where five or more metal cations are simultaneously incorporated into a single crystallographic phase.

The synthesis was carried out via the sol-gel route, chosen for its ability to achieve atomic-level homogeneity at low processing temperatures and relatively low cost.

Structural and functional characterization formed the heart of this work. X-ray diffraction with Rietveld refinement confirmed single-phase formation. FESEM with EDS mapping verified spatial homogeneity. UV-Vis spectroscopy was used to extract the optical bandgap, while FTIR confirmed metal-oxygen bonding.

Student Guide — For Indian Students

I went from a regular college to a PhD seat at National Taiwan University — without clearing IELTS, without a famous undergraduate institution, and without knowing anyone abroad. This guide is everything I wish someone had told me.

India is at an inflection point in its semiconductor journey. The India Semiconductor Mission is channeling billions into fabrication plants, backed by companies like Tata Electronics and Micron. The demand for trained engineers far exceeds supply.

Semiconductor engineering demands genuine expertise — you cannot fake solid-state physics or device operation knowledge. This high entry barrier is your shield once you have built expertise. A Physics or Electronics graduate who commits to this field has a legitimate path to global opportunities.

The cold email is the most underused tool available to Indian students. Most students either never send one, or send generic templates. The difference is specificity — read the professor's work before writing.

Open by naming a specific paper, explain what connected with your thinking, then describe your relevant work. Keep it under 200 words. Attach a one-page CV. Use proper titles, not "Respected Sir/Madam". Send personalized emails, never mass mail. Follow up once after two weeks.

Your CV is a curated argument for why a professor should invest time in you. The most important thing is your thesis or research project — describe it specifically with what you did, techniques used, and results.

List only tools you actually use: MATLAB, LaTeX, Origin, Python, specific equipment. Include Coursera certifications if from reputable universities and relevant. Keep to one page unless you have publications. Use LaTeX for typesetting — it signals care and precision to professors.

Start with research questions, not university rankings. Identify topics of interest, then use Google Scholar to find active researchers publishing in the last three years. Recent activity matters more than historical prestige.

Read their group website carefully. Look for open positions, funding sources, and whether students' work overlaps with yours. Check LinkedIn to see where alumni went — this reveals mentorship quality and career outcomes. Reach out to current students if possible.

Many research programs, especially in Taiwan, South Korea, Japan, and Europe, waive English language tests or accept alternatives. In Taiwan, universities like NTU waive IELTS/TOEFL if your education was in English, which is true for most Indian students. Get a letter from your institution confirming medium of instruction.

Check if they accept Duolingo English Test — significantly cheaper and faster. Some programs waive requirements entirely for specific fellowships like TEEP, where research ability matters more than test scores.

Taiwan's TEEP (Taiwan Experience Education Program) is the most accessible research internship for Indian students in semiconductors and materials science. Fully funded including flight, accommodation, and monthly stipend. Places you in a Taiwanese lab for 3-6 months. Evaluated on research background and professor match. This led directly to my PhD at NTU.

Within India, INUP workshops at IITs and IISc offer hands-on cleanroom and characterization experience. Apply early — even as an M.Sc. student — to build skills and track record. The IISc winter school at CeNSE is rigorous; scoring well is worth noting on your CV.

MATLAB is the lingua franca of signal processing and optical simulation — know it well enough to write scripts, not just run existing ones. Python with NumPy, SciPy, and Matplotlib is equally essential, especially for AI or data-driven methods.

Origin is standard for scientific data plotting and fitting — learn it properly including curve fitting. LaTeX is non-negotiable for papers, theses, and proposals. Professors notice well-typeset documents — it signals care and precision. Start learning it during your M.Sc., not your thesis deadline.
