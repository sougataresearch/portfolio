About Me

A brief overview for supervisors, collaborators and industry partners.

I completed my M.Sc. in Electronics Science at Jadavpur University, where I worked on the synthesis and characterization of lead-free high-entropy perovskite oxides using sol–gel techniques. My training combines semiconductor device fundamentals, advanced materials characterization and scientific data analysis using Python and MATLAB.

As a TEEP Research Intern at the College of Semiconductor Research, National Tsing Hua University, I work on semiconductor material synthesis routes, process flow integration and the relationship between growth conditions, material properties and device performance. This experience bridges lab-scale experimentation with practical semiconductor process thinking.

From February 2026, I will pursue a PhD at National Taiwan University in Mechanical Engineering (Nanoengineering & Nanoscience), focusing on semiconductor metrology and instrumentation. My long-term goal is to contribute to high-precision measurement systems and inspection tools for advanced semiconductor manufacturing.

Research Focus

Key themes I am interested in developing during my PhD and beyond.

Optical Metrology & 3D Surface Profiling
White-light interferometry, wafer and device inspection
  • Development and optimization of white-light interferometry (WLI) systems for high-precision 3D surface profiling.
  • Thin-film thickness measurement and surface defect detection for semiconductor and MEMS devices.
  • Integration of optical setups with precision stages and detectors for robust measurement workflows.
WLI 3D profiling Thin films Semiconductor inspection
Computational Imaging & Signal Processing
Physics-based modeling for metrology
  • Phase retrieval and axial resolution enhancement for complex and high-aspect-ratio surfaces.
  • Numerical modeling of optical systems to understand trade-offs in design and performance.
  • Data processing pipelines in Python/MATLAB for interferometric and imaging measurements.
Nanomaterials & Device Characterization
High-entropy oxides and thin-film systems
  • Sol–gel synthesis and characterization of lead-free high-entropy perovskite oxides.
  • Use of XRD, FESEM/EDS, UV–Vis, FTIR, TGA/DSC to correlate structure, morphology and optical properties.
  • Exploring links between material properties and device-relevant metrics for thermoelectric and related applications.
